The Dehiwala Zoo is widely regarded as the main tourist attraction in Sri Lanka for flora and fauna.
It's definitely a must to visit at least once!

Various animals are showcased inside exhibits and artificial enclosures, tailored to suit their respective needs and provide a comfortable, safe space for these creatures to thrive in.

Whilst the main focus of the zoo is to showcase the local wildlife to foreigners and locals alike, the diversity of fauna knows no bounds with numerous animals varying from macaws to flamingos to even a butterfly garden.

About the Zoo

The Dehiwala National Zoo, which has a remarkable collection of exotic and indigenous fauna, is one of the oldest Zoos in Asia. The Zoo in Sri Lanka is widely known as Colombo Zoo all over the world.

Annually, over one and half million local and foreign tourists visit the Zoo. And over 2000 students and groups of students from schools and universities visit the Zoo for educational purposes.

The Zoo is trying to keep the pace with the changing world. It has been involved in some major redevelopment programmes with the aim of enhancing the overall image of the Zoo while providing maximum facilities for animals and the visitors.

About our wildlife

Currently, the Dehiwala Zoo houses :

  • 72 species of mammals
  • 65 species of birds
  • 31 species of reptiles
  • 89 species of fish
  • 03 families of amphibians
  • 30 species of butterflies, etc...

The total number of animals varies from 2500-3000 due to breeding and free living colonies of birds.
Dehiwala Zoo has become famous amongst visitors not only for collection of local and exotic animals
but also for landscaping with lush greeneries.
The Zoo supports and promotes conservation by breeding certain rare and endangered species through proper animal welfare.
