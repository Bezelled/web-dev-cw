The Dehiwala Zoo is widely regarded as the main tourist attraction in Sri Lanka for flora and fauna.
It's definitely a must to visit at least once!

Various animals are showcased inside exhibits and artificial enclosures, tailored to suit their respective needs and provide a comfortable, safe space for these creatures to thrive in.

Whilst the main focus of the zoo is to showcase the local wildlife to foreigners and locals alike, the diversity of fauna knows no bounds with numerous animals varying from macaws to flamingos to even a butterfly garden.

Ecological Importance

The Zoo is constantly contributing to advances in the field of wildlife, medicine and agriculture.
Not limited to just to exhibiting exotic wildlife, the zoo is a pinnacle of Sri Lankan wildlife preservation :
  • It has an Education Centre, where (upon prior notice) professionals teach students and visitors on various activities and the importance of its inhabitants.
  • Its Research Centre has contributed over 45 new findings to the National Department of Wildlife in the past 5 years alone.
  • Was noted at the UN Summit of 2019 for being a highly advanced wildlife centre with several major advancements that aided foreign scientists.
  • Contains many extinct-in-the-wild flora and fauna that prove to be the last remaining members of their species.
    Attempts are being made to try and breed or preserve these great species for longer.
